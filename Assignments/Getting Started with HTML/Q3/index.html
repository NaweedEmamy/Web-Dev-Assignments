Starfield maker on 'choice anxiety', long games and exclusive titles

Todd Howard, game director of Skyrim and Fallout 4, is speaking to the BBC ahead of the launch of his most ambitious project to date. Starfield is a sprawling space epic, giving players the freedom to navigate the stars in search of action and adventure. Howard admits candidly that this new release, "ended up being much larger than anything we've done. At times we thought, 'are we in over our heads?'" It's not surprising, given the scope of the game Howard and his team were aiming to create.

But how long is too long?

It is a question that is often asked in gaming lobbies, Whatsapp chats or in pubs and coffee shops.Aren't shorter, more focused and polished experiences the way forward? Do all modern titles need hundreds of hours worth of material? Doesn't that risk the inclusion of errors - all for the sake of providing untold options for players to choose between - when the majority of players won't access all the options? Howard isn't concerned:
